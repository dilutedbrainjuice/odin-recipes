Mapo Tofu

Description

A traditional Sichuan dish, mapo tofu is made with simmered medium-firm silken tofu flavored with fermented bean paste, beef, plenty of red-hot roasted chili oil, and a handful of Sichuan peppercorns

Ingredients:

  • Tofu - firm or extra-firm, cut into cubes
  • Ground pork or beef (optional)
  • Cooking Oil
  • Garlic, minced
  • Ginger, minced
  • Green onions, chopped (for garnish)
  • Sichuan peppercorns (optional, for a numbing sensation)
  • Doubanjiang (fermented broad bean paste with chili)
  • Soy sauce
  • Shaoxing wine (or rice wine)
  • Chicken or vegetable broth
  • Cornstarch (mixed with water to make a slurry for thickening)
  • Red chili flakes or powder (adjust to taste for spice level)
  • Sugar
  • Salt (adjust to taste)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the ingredients listed above
  2. Cook the ground meat (if using) in a wok or pan with oil until browned. Set aside.
  3. In the same wok or pan, sauté garlic, ginger, and green onions until fragrant.
  4. Add Doubanjiang and Sichuan peppercorns, sauté for additional flavor.
  5. Add tofu cubes and stir gently to coat with the mixture.
  6. Pour in soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, and chicken or vegetable broth. Simmer until tofu is cooked through.
  7. Stir in the cooked ground meat (if using).
  8. Thicken the sauce with a cornstarch slurry.
  9. Season with red chili flakes or powder, sugar, and salt to taste.
  10. Serve hot, garnished with chopped green onions.
